My Interests in Biochemistry

Starting Out

My interests began with molecular models. They looked like this:

A recreation of a possible past mistake...

Back in the day, these were the version of "LEGOs" that I was allowed to play with while at my dad's office. I learned about chemical reactions and the periodic table while I was at it. I remember putting together 6 water , 6 oxygen, 6 carbon dioxide, and a "glucose" molecule
(I didn't know about stereochemistry at the time and this is what I called whatever I put together following the bond-line of glucose so I don't actually know what I put together now)

Enter Biology...

During fourth grade, I found this book called Human Anatomy and The Molecular Biology of the Cell. Here, I began my journey into biochemistry. Reading these books, I realized that the basis of how cells and, by extension, the entire human body, was all from chemistry. At this point I could understand balancing reactions, but actual chemical mechanisms. As I started to try and understand the pathways underlying metabolism as outlined in The Molecular Biology of the Cell, I realized I was missing a lot of the reasoning and logic behind why all these pathways need to go through those intricate steps and my progress went on a hiatus.

The key: Organic Chemistry

About two-thirds of my way through organic chemistry, I started to connect back to the reactions I saw in The Molecular Biology of the Cell (I kind of treat it as my biochem bible). The carbonyl and heterocycle chemistry really connected and explained the steps required in glycolysis and the Citric Acid Cycle. At the same time, COVID-19 caused my last third of chemistry to be online, and so I did the natural thing and turned in all my homework in Chemdraw. For some reason. Exempla gratia:

This was a practice synthesis problem

Now for some actual biochemistry...

During the actual biochemistry series I enjoyed how the chemistry connected some reasoning behind some metabolic processes to normal bodily function and disease. Connecting all this knowledge together was quite rewarding. Not to mention, I started to enjoy making some of these chemdraw figures...

Following carbons around the citric acid cycle~

Summary

Essentially, what I am interested in is how the chemistry and interactions occurring in the metabolome can directly change how the body works whether this is at a cellular, systemic, or organismal scale.
