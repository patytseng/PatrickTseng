Research Experience

Yu Lab

At the Yu Lab, my contribution was in a project concerning a palladium catalyzed cross-coupling reaction. Much of the time spent here was in benchwork and assisting the postdoc researcher I was assigned to. During this experience, I was exposed to chemistry techniques including HPLC, Silica Column Chromatography, NMR, reactions under inert gas or vacuum, and general chemistry lab maintenance work.

Zhu Lab

At the Zhu Lab, my contribution was in a project that researched a device for both diagnostic and disease monitoring purposes. I participated in dry lab work involving analysis of fluorescence microscopy imaging results (locating CTCs out of a WBC background) as well as wet lab work involving the experimental procedure of running their device. Parts of this workflow included centrifugation, ultracentrifugation, qPCR/ddPCR/RT-PCR, and lots of pipetting. During this, I also created an inventory tracking system for frozen blood and tissue samples and created an animation of the mechanism behind the researched diagnostic device. On top of these laboratory activities, I also actively participated in the manuscript revision process for both publications I have a name in.
