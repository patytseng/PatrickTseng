Critique

J. B. Miller, S. Zhang, P. Kos, H. Xiong, K. Zhou, S. S. Perelman, H. Zhu, D. J. Siegwart, Angew. Chemie 2017, 129, 1079.

My thoughts on:
"Non-Viral CRISPR/Cas Gene Editing In Vitro and In Vivo Enabled by Synthetic Nanoparticle Co-Delivery of Cas9 mRNA and sgRNA"

This research group used a library of 72 zwitterionic amino lipids (ZALs) in two different categories (ZAx-Epn and ZAx-Acn) to encapsulate Cas9 mRNA. They referred to these nanoparticles as zwitterionic amino lipid nanoparticles (ZNPs). Out of these 72 zwitterionic amino lipids, the lipid ZA3-Ep10 was the best in in vitro studies and was chosen for usage in an in vivo study to determine the effectiveness of this overall technique. Administration of ZA3-Ep10 into genetically engineered mice (with homozygous Rosa26 promoter Lox‐Stop‐Lox tdTomato, tdTO cassette) introduced a CRISPR/Cas9 system in the liver and kidney. The CRISPR/Cas9 system was designed to disrupt two LoxP locations to remove the stop cassette and subsequent non-homologous end joining (NHEJ) occurred to express fluorescent signals of the tdTomato protein.

Overall for early forms of CRISPR/Cas9 delivery by nanoparticle, this looks promising. However, seeing as this technology was published in 2016, more recent developments of lipid nanoparticles has brought out more targetability or efficiency in delivery editing or loading. Using the tdTomato protein as a fluorescent probe is a very solid way to evaluate gene editing.

However, looking at the mechanism of how lipid nanoparticles are "specifically" targeting the liver and kidneys, it is evident that these lipid nanoparticles can't target other tissues with this same mechanism. While not described in this publication, lipid nanoparticles use their similarities to biological lipid particles to be absorbed into the liver. This would mean that there needs more development before this technology can become more generalized across more cells. More recent focus in this area has created tunable surface properties for more controllable targeting of different tissues as in here.

The publication didn’t cover many topics like how the nanoparticle was uptaken by cells or how the endosomal escape pathway may have occurred, but it stands to reason that this was never the focus of the article or the journal.
